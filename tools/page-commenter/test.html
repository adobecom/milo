The Challenge
Your campaigns are slower
than your customers

Every time a user views a product, launches an app, or starts a checkout, that action lands on their profile as a raw event. Thousands of them. By the time your campaign evaluates all that data, the moment has passed.

"We wanted to target users who browsed Photoshop in the last week. The segment took so long to evaluate at the edge that the offer rendered after they'd already left the page."
🐢
Campaigns miss the moment
Heavy profiles slow edge evaluation. Personalization that should fire in milliseconds arrives too late — after the user has already moved on.
📦
You're burning profile space
Every stored event counts against your entitlements. You're forced to choose: keep 90 days of behavioral history, or stay within license limits.
🧩
Simple questions, complex setup
"Has this user viewed a product page recently?" should be a checkbox — not a custom PQL expression that requires engineering support to build.
Sound familiar?

Good to Know
A few things to keep
in mind
01
Scope
Boolean traits (true/false) are available now. Frequency, value, and composite traits are coming in future releases.
Available
Expanding
02
Data
When you enable traits for a dataset, raw events are no longer stored on the profile. The trait replaces them — that's how the size savings work.
By design
03

Each trait has a configurable time-to-live from 1 to 120 days. After expiry, the trait clears automatically — no manual cleanup.
Flexible
